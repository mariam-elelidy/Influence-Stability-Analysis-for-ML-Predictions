CROSS-RESULT OBSERVATIONS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. Influence != leverage alone, and != residual alone. Point #2 (idx=287)
   has the largest residual in the dataset (1.969) but lowest leverage in
   top-10 (0.014) and ranks 2nd. Neither metric in isolation gives the
   correct ranking. The joint product r_i^2 * h_ii / (1-h_ii)^2 is required.

2. Negative ΔRMSE at K=10 and K=25 is diagnostic, not a cleaning signal.
   These points are hard-to-fit but legitimate. Removing them makes the
   remaining problem easier — the model is doing its job on the hardest cases.

3. The random-removal max (0.028 across 100 trials) never reaches the
   influential mean (0.032). Influence scores identify structurally leveraged
   points, not just high-residual ones. This validates the score as a
   stability diagnostic rather than a residual filter.

4. Marginal returns diminish past K=25. K=100 produces max|Delta|=0.128 vs
   K=25 max|Delta|=0.120 — only 6% worse despite removing 4× as many points.
   The influence mass is concentrated; the bound is essentially set by K~25.
